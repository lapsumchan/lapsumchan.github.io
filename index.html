I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Biostatistics at the University of Minnesota School of Public Health, under the supervision of Professor Wei Pan. My work centers on pioneering statistical approaches for omics data, including GWAS, microbiome, and metabolomics. In terms of methods development, my research interests are high-dimensional statistics and causal inference. Recently, I have pivoted to developing robust Mendelian randomization methods, using genetic variants to estimate the causal effects of exposures on health outcomes. Currently, I am utilizing UK Biobank data to identify metabolites causally linked to Alzheimer's disease and atrial fibrillation.
